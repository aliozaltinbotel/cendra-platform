# Airbnb Damage Claim Rules & Guidelines

## The 24-Hour Rule
- All damage claims **MUST** be submitted within **24 hours** of the guest's checkout time
- Claims **MUST** be submitted **before the next guest checks in**
- The earlier of these two deadlines applies
- Late claims are almost always denied regardless of evidence quality

## Claim Submission Process

### Step 1: Document the Damage (Immediately upon discovery)
1. Take clear, well-lit photos of ALL damage
2. Include wide-angle shots for context and close-ups for detail
3. Take photos of the same areas from your "before" photo set for comparison
4. Note the exact time of discovery
5. Do NOT clean or repair anything until fully documented

### Step 2: Contact the Guest (Within 1 hour)
1. Send a message through the Airbnb platform (creates a paper trail)
2. Be factual and non-accusatory: "During our post-checkout inspection, we noticed..."
3. Ask if they are aware of the damage
4. Give them an opportunity to respond before filing

### Step 3: Get Repair Estimates (Within 4 hours)
1. Contact at least one licensed professional for a written estimate
2. Include labor and materials costs
3. For items requiring replacement, include original purchase receipts if available
4. Apply reasonable depreciation for items over 2 years old

### Step 4: File the Claim (Within 24 hours)
1. Go to the Airbnb Resolution Center
2. Select the booking and "Request money"
3. Upload all documentation:
   - Before photos (with timestamps)
   - After photos (with timestamps)
   - Repair estimates
   - Receipts for original purchases
   - Written description of damage
4. Submit the exact amount — do not inflate or round up

## Required Documentation Checklist
- [ ] Before photos (pre-stay condition)
- [ ] After photos (post-stay damage)
- [ ] Detailed damage description
- [ ] Repair estimate from professional
- [ ] Host statement (factual narrative)
- [ ] Original purchase receipts (if claiming replacement)
- [ ] Guest communication log (Airbnb messages)
- [ ] Cleaning crew damage report (if they discovered it)

## What Is Claimable
- **Physical damage**: Broken furniture, scratched floors, stained carpets
- **Missing items**: Stolen or removed property items
- **Excessive cleaning**: Beyond normal turnover (e.g., pet damage, smoking)
- **Key/lock replacement**: If keys are lost or locks are damaged

## What Is NOT Claimable
- **Normal wear and tear**: Minor scuffs, faded fabrics, loose handles
- **Pre-existing damage**: Anything visible in your before photos
- **Consumables**: Used toiletries, coffee, cleaning supplies
- **Emotional distress**: Non-tangible costs
- **Lost revenue**: Cancellations due to damage (separate claim process)

## AirCover for Hosts
Airbnb's Host Damage Protection covers up to $3,000,000 per incident:
- Damage to property and belongings
- Required deep cleaning costs
- Lost income if property is uninhabitable during repairs
- Pet damage (if pets were not authorized)

### AirCover Process
1. File through Resolution Center first
2. If guest does not respond or denies within 72 hours, escalate to Airbnb
3. Airbnb assigns a case manager
4. Case manager reviews evidence and makes a determination
5. Typical processing time: 5-10 business days
6. Payment is issued to the host's payout method

## Tips for Successful Claims
1. **Always have before photos** — claims without baseline photos are rarely approved
2. **Submit early** — don't wait until the deadline
3. **Be specific** — vague descriptions reduce credibility
4. **Be honest** — inflated claims risk account penalties
5. **Respond quickly** — Airbnb may ask follow-up questions within 24 hours
6. **Keep communication on-platform** — off-platform messages are not considered evidence
