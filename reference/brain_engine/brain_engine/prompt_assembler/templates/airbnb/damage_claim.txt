AIRBNB DAMAGE CLAIM REQUIREMENTS:

1. THE 24-HOUR RULE
   - Claims MUST be submitted within 24 hours of the guest's checkout
   - Claims MUST be submitted BEFORE the next guest checks in
   - Late claims are almost always denied — this is the #1 reason for rejection
   - Document the exact checkout time and calculate the deadline

2. REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION CHECKLIST
   □ Before photos (pre-stay condition, timestamped)
   □ After photos (post-stay damage, timestamped)
   □ Detailed damage description (location, type, extent)
   □ Repair estimate or replacement cost (from vendor if possible)
   □ Host statement (factual, non-emotional narrative)
   □ Receipt or proof of original purchase (if claiming replacement cost)
   □ Communication log with guest (if damage was discussed)

3. CLAIM DOCUMENT FORMAT

   Subject: Damage Claim - Booking [BOOKING_ID] - [GUEST_NAME]

   SECTION 1: INCIDENT SUMMARY
   - Property: [Address]
   - Guest: [Name]
   - Check-in: [Date/Time]
   - Check-out: [Date/Time]
   - Damage discovered: [Date/Time]

   SECTION 2: DAMAGE DESCRIPTION
   For each damaged item:
   - Item: [Description]
   - Location: [Where in the property]
   - Condition before stay: [Description + reference to before photos]
   - Condition after stay: [Description + reference to after photos]
   - Type of damage: [Stain/scratch/break/etc.]

   SECTION 3: COST BREAKDOWN
   | Item | Repair/Replace | Cost | Evidence |
   |------|---------------|------|----------|
   | [Item] | [R or R] | $[Amount] | [Photo ref] |
   | TOTAL | | $[Total] | |

   SECTION 4: HOST STATEMENT
   [Factual, chronological narrative of events]

   SECTION 5: EVIDENCE INDEX
   - Photo 1: [Description, timestamp]
   - Photo 2: [Description, timestamp]
   - Receipt 1: [Description]

4. WRITING THE HOST STATEMENT
   - Be factual and chronological — avoid emotional language
   - State what you observed, when you observed it, and what action you took
   - Reference specific evidence (photos, messages) by number
   - Do NOT accuse the guest of intentional damage
   - Focus on the condition change between check-in and check-out

5. COST GUIDELINES
   - Claim replacement cost only if repair is not feasible
   - Apply depreciation for items over 2 years old
   - Airbnb typically covers actual cost, not retail price
   - Include labor costs for professional repairs
   - Get at least one written estimate from a licensed professional

6. SUBMISSION TIPS
   - Submit through the Resolution Center, not customer support
   - Request the exact amount — do not round up or inflate
   - Respond to Airbnb's follow-up questions within 24 hours
   - Keep all communication professional and documented

NEVER:
- Inflate damage costs or exaggerate the extent of damage
- Include pre-existing damage in the claim
- Use emotional or accusatory language
- Submit without photo evidence
- Miss the 24-hour deadline
